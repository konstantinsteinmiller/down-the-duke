## Player Mechanics

**Health**
The Player’s health begins each level with 5 HP.
Health does not regenerate.
Can be regained from health pickups.
Player loses when health reaches 0
Low Health is indicated by visual changes: cracked cannon, splintered railings, a gradual darkening of the wood railing.

**Cannonball Ammo**
Player has unlimited ammo
A reload takes 0.75 seconds

**Standard Shot**
Tap to fire
Damage: 1 hit point (can be defined however you like)
Speed: 1 speed (can be defined however you like)
Intercepts black and red shots

**Charged Shot**
Double tap to fire for double damage (2x ball damage, 1.25x ball speed)
There is a 0.5 delay until the charged shot is fired and the player cant fire normal shots in between, but move the
cannon.
Rips through black enemy shots

**Enemy Cannon Mechanics**
An enemy cannon will glint or flash to indicate an attack after reloading. A special case is made for the first attacker
on Phase 2 of Level 1 as they must flash to indicate an imminent attack.

**Black Cannonballs (Black Shots)**
Standard enemy projectile: -1 HP damage
Impacts the player in 2.25 seconds
Explodes if intercepted mid-air

**Red Cannonballs (Red Shots)**
Slow movement
Impacts the player in 4 seconds
Fierce mid-air wobbling (think Kirby cannons)
Can be deflected back at enemies

**Cannonball Trajectory**
The trajectory of an enemy cannonball is situated on a 30-degree angle and flies on an arc, so it is easily spotted
on-screen. The trajectory should be based on the side the enemy cannons are firing from. For example: if an enemy is on
the left-hand side, they’ll fire anywhere between the middle to the right-hand side. If the enemy is on the right-hand
side, they’ll fire anywhere between the middle to the left-hand side.
